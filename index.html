Bilal Al Taki

Industrial Career

I am a Project Manager at Capgemini Engineering, where I spearhead the SEANERGIES project, dedicated to developing floating datacenters in the ocean. This project encompasses various research areas, including hydrodynamic stability, mechanical conception, thermodynamics, digital-twin technology, renewable energy, and mechanical calculus. My work focuses on creating innovative solutions to optimize the performance and sustainability of these datacenters.

Academic Background

I did my Ph.D. thesis at the University of Grenoble-Alpes and the Lebanese University under the supervision of Didier Bresch and Raafat Talhouk, which I defended on December 19, 2016.

My research interests are in the analysis of nonlinear partial differential equations, particularly those related to fluid mechanics. Key areas of my research include incompressible and compressible fluids, degenerate elliptic equations, Newtonian and non-Newtonian fluids, and functional inequalities.

I am also a part-time teacher at Pôle Léonard de Vinci, where I share my expertise in these fields with the next generation of researchers and engineers.

Interests in AI and Data Science

In addition to my work in fluid mechanics, I have accumulated significant experience in AI and data science. I am deeply interested in exploring the mathematical challenges and real-world problems that AI can address. This includes understanding how AI can be applied to complex systems and finding innovative solutions to practical problems.
