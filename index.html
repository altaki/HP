Bilal Al Taki

Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics from the University of Grenoble-Alpes and the Lebanese University. My research focuses on partial differential equations (PDEs) and their applications to fluid dynamics, including degenerate and non-Newtonian models, low-Mach regimes, and numerical methods for Navier–Stokes systems. I have published in leading journals such as Journal of Differential Equations and Nonlinearity.

Currently, I am an Adjunct Lecturer at ESILV and EMLV (Pôle Léonard de Vinci, Paris), teaching applied mathematics and engineering courses. In parallel, I contribute to research-driven innovation as a Lead Research Scientist at Capgemini Engineering, where I define research directions on energy-efficient cooling systems and hydrodynamic stability for floating data centers—a project recognized as a finalist in the Grand Prix de l’Ingénierie.

My goal is to bridge theoretical advances in PDEs with computational modeling and sustainable technologies, fostering collaborations that connect academia and industry.

Research Interest

As a Project Manager at Capgemini Engineering, I lead multidisciplinary teams in developing innovative solutions for complex challenges. My expertise spans from project conceptualization to execution, with a strong emphasis on integrating advanced mathematical and computational techniques into practical applications.

Academic Background

Currently, I am actively engaged as a part-time lecturer at ESILV and EMLV (Pôle Léonard de Vinci, Paris), teaching various courses across different domains. My research during my academic career was devoted to the study of nonlinear differential equations, particularly their applications in fluid mechanics and related fields, contributing to advancements in understanding complex phenomena.
